Understanding Security Cheques in Indian Law

               

A security cheque is generally understood as a cheque issued by a borrower to a lender as a collateral or guarantee for the fulfillment of a future financial obligation or for the performance of a contract. Unlike cheques issued for an existing debt, the liability associated with a security cheque is often contingent upon the occurrence of a future event or the failure of the drawer to meet their obligations.

Section 139 of the Negotiable Instruments Act & the Presumption of Debt or Liability

               

Section 139 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, states that unless the contrary is proved, it shall be presumed that the holder of a cheque received the cheque for the discharge, in whole or in part, of any debt or other liability. This section creates a rebuttable presumption in favor of the holder of the cheque.
